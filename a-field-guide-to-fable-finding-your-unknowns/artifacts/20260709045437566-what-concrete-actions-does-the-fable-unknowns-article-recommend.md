The tangible moves are: 1. Ask for a blindspot pass before starting unfamiliar work. 2. Make brainstorms and quick prototypes when you do not know what you want until you see it. 3. Have the agent interview you one question at a time about ambiguous decisions. 4. Give references, especially source code, when words are not enough. 5. Ask for an implementation plan that surfaces tweakable decisions first: data models, interfaces, UX flows. 6. During implementation, keep implementation-notes.md so deviations and edge cases are logged. 7. After implementation, create pitch/explainer artifacts for reviewers. 8. Ask for a quiz or change report before merging so you can prove you understand what changed. The common pattern: turn vague uncertainty into an artifact you can react to, review, or learn from.
